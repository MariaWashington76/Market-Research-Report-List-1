<p><p>The rare hematology treatment market is primarily segmented into two categories: plasma-derived factors and recombinant factors. Plasma-derived factors are obtained from human plasma and are used to treat conditions like hemophilia, providing essential clotting proteins. In contrast, recombinant factors are synthetically produced through biotechnological methods, offering a safer alternative with reduced risk of viral transmission. Both categories aim to manage bleeding disorders, improve patient outcomes, and enhance the quality of life for individuals with rare hematological conditions.</p></p>
