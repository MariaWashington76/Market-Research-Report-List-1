<p><p>The vegetarian capsule materials market primarily features three types: Hydroxypropyl Methyl Cellulose (HPMC), Pullulan, and Starch. HPMC is a cellulose derivative commonly used for its biodegradability and stability, making it popular for encapsulating dietary supplements. Pullulan, derived from fermentation, offers low moisture absorption and is ideal for heat-sensitive ingredients. Starch-based capsules, often derived from corn or potatoes, are recognized for their cost-effectiveness and eco-friendliness. Together, these materials cater to the growing demand for vegetarian and vegan-friendly capsule options.</p></p>
