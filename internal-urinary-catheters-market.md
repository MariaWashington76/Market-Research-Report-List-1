<p><ul><li>Indwelling Catheters</li><li>Intermittent Catheters</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The internal urinary catheters market comprises two main types: indwelling catheters and intermittent catheters. Indwelling catheters, also known as Foley catheters, are designed for long-term use and remain inserted in the bladder, draining urine continuously. Intermittent catheters, on the other hand, are used temporarily and are inserted periodically to drain urine before being removed. Both types serve essential roles in managing urinary retention and incontinence, each catering to specific patient needs and conditions, ultimately enhancing comfort and quality of life.</p></p>
