<p><p>Continuous Subcutaneous Insulin Infusion (CSII) devices are advanced tools for diabetes management, designed to deliver insulin continuously. The market is primarily divided into two types: normal pumps and patch pumps. Normal pumps are traditional devices that use a tethered catheter to deliver insulin where users can program insulin delivery patterns. Patch pumps, on the other hand, are portable, wireless units that adhere directly to the skin, offering more flexibility and ease of use without the need for external tubing.</p></p>
