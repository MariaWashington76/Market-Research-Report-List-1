<p><p>The livestock animal vaccines market comprises various types, including live attenuated vaccines, inactivated vaccines, and other vaccine forms. Live attenuated vaccines use weakened pathogens to stimulate an immune response without causing disease. Inactivated vaccines contain killed pathogens, offering safety and stability but often requiring boosters. Other vaccines may include subunit, recombinant, or DNA vaccines, which utilize specific components of pathogens to elicit immunity. Each type serves distinct needs in livestock health management, enhancing disease prevention and overall productivity.</p></p>
