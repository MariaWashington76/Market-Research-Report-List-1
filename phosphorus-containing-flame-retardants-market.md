<p><p>Phosphorus-containing flame retardants are categorized into inorganic and organic types based on their chemical structures and properties. Inorganic flame retardants, such as ammonium polyphosphate, offer high thermal stability and effectiveness but may require higher concentrations. Organic flame retardants, like phosphonates and phosphinates, provide efficient flame suppression and often improve mechanical properties of materials. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing fire safety across various applications, including textiles, electronics, and construction materials, driven by regulatory demands and safety standards.</p></p>
