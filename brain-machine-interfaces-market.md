<p><ul><li>Invasive</li><li>Non-Invasive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Brain Machine Interfaces (BMI) market is categorized into invasive and non-invasive types. Invasive BMIs involve surgical implantation into the brain, offering high-resolution signals for applications like neuroprosthetics and advanced neurological research. Conversely, non-invasive BMIs utilize external devices, such as EEG caps or headsets, to monitor brain activity without surgery. While non-invasive systems provide safer, easier-to-use solutions for applications like gaming and rehabilitation, invasive systems usually deliver more precise control for medical applications, enhancing user interaction with technology.</p></p>
