<p><strong>The Vitamin D Tests Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>RIA</li><li>ELISA</li><li>CLIA</li><li>POCT</li><li>LC-MS/MS</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Vitamin D tests market encompasses various testing methods, each with unique characteristics. Radioimmunoassay (RIA) offers high sensitivity but involves radioactive materials. Enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA) is widely used for its simplicity and cost-effectiveness. Chemiluminescent immunoassays (CLIA) provide rapid results with enhanced sensitivity. Point-of-care testing (POCT) enables immediate testing in clinical settings, enhancing patient convenience. Liquid chromatography-tandem mass spectrometry (LC-MS/MS) offers precise quantification of vitamin D metabolites, ensuring accuracy in clinical and research applications.</p></p>

<p><p>The Vitamin D tests market includes two primary applications: the 25-hydroxyvitamin D test (25(OH)D) and the 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D test (1,25(OH)2D). The 25(OH)D test measures the total vitamin D levels in the body, crucial for assessing deficiencies and monitoring supplementation, while the 1,25(OH)2D test evaluates the active form of vitamin D, important for diagnosing specific disorders like renal disease and certain bone disorders. Both tests are essential for managing health conditions related to vitamin D levels.</p></p>
