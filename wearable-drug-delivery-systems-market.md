<p><p>Wearable drug delivery systems encompass two primary market types: electronic and mechanical devices. Electronic drug delivery devices utilize advanced technology, such as sensors and microcontrollers, to administer medication with precision and flexibility, often allowing for programmable dosing. In contrast, mechanical drug delivery devices rely on simple mechanics, such as pumps or syringes, to release drugs in a controlled manner without electronic components. Both types enhance patient compliance and convenience, offering innovative solutions in personalized medicine and chronic disease management.</p></p>
