<p><p>The over-the-counter (OTC) pain medication market comprises three primary types: oral, topical, and parenteral. Oral medications, such as tablets and capsules, are widely used for systemic pain relief and convenience. Topical treatments, including creams and gels, are applied directly to the skin, targeting localized pain with minimal systemic effects. Parenteral medications, typically in injectable form, provide rapid pain relief for acute conditions, allowing for direct delivery into the bloodstream. Each type caters to different patient needs and preferences.</p></p>
