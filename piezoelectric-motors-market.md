<p><p>The piezoelectric motors market is primarily divided into AC and DC piezoelectric motors. AC piezoelectric motors utilize alternating current to drive piezoelectric materials, allowing for high-speed operation and precision control, often suited for applications like robotics and micro-manipulation. In contrast, DC piezoelectric motors operate on direct current, providing better torque at lower speeds, making them ideal for applications requiring high torque and low power consumption, such as in medical devices and optical systems. Both types offer unique advantages tailored to specific industry needs.</p></p>
