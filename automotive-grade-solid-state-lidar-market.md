<p><p>The Automotive Grade Solid-State LiDAR market is categorized into Hybrid Solid-State and All Solid-State types. Hybrid Solid-State LiDAR combines mechanical and solid-state components, offering a balance between performance and cost, suitable for semi-autonomous and advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS). All Solid-State LiDAR, entirely free of moving parts, provides higher durability, compactness, and reliability, ideal for fully autonomous vehicles. Both types enhance safety and perception in automotive applications, with All Solid-State being more advanced and costlier, while Hybrid offers a transitional solution.</p></p>
