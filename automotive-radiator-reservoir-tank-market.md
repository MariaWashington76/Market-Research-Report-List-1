<p><p>The automotive radiator reservoir tank market is divided into two main types: pressurized and non-pressurized tanks. Pressurized tanks are designed to withstand high-pressure conditions, enabling them to efficiently manage coolant flow and temperature variations within the cooling system. Non-pressurized tanks, on the other hand, operate at ambient pressure, primarily serving as overflow and coolant storage without pressurization. Both types play crucial roles in maintaining optimal engine performance and preventing overheating, catering to different vehicle requirements and cooling system designs.</p></p>
