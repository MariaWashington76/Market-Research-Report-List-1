<p><p>3D Arterial Spin Labeling (3D ASL) is a non-invasive MRI technique that measures cerebral blood flow. The market is primarily segmented into Continuous Arterial Spin Labeling (CASL) and Pulsed Arterial Spin Labeling (PASL). CASL utilizes a continuous flow of radiofrequency pulses to label arterial blood, providing high spatial resolution and sensitivity. In contrast, PASL employs short, pulsed labeling, offering simpler implementations and flexibility. Both methods are integral in diagnosing neurological disorders and assessing brain function.</p></p>
