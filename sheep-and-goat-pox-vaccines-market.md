Live vaccines contain weakened forms of the virus, providing a strong immune response and long-lasting protection, often requiring fewer doses. In contrast, killed vaccines use inactivated virus, ensuring safety and stability, but typically necessitate multiple doses for effective immunity. Both types are essential in preventing outbreaks of pox diseases in sheep and goats, safeguarding livestock health and supporting the agricultural economy.</p></p>
