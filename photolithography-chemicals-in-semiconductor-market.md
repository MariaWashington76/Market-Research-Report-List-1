<p><p>Photolithography chemicals are essential for the semiconductor market, enabling the precise patterning of circuits on silicon wafers. Key types include silicon wafers, which serve as the substrate for semiconductor devices; photoresist, a light-sensitive material used to transfer patterns; and HMDS (Hexamethyldisilazane), a spin-on primer that enhances photoresist adhesion. Additionally, photoresist ancillaries, such as developers and strippers, support the photolithography process by facilitating pattern development and removal. Together, these components drive the advancement of semiconductor technology.</p></p>
