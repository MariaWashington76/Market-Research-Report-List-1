<p><p>Synthetic polyisoprene rubber is primarily classified into two market types based on the configuration of the isoprene units: cis and trans. The cis configuration, which dominates the health and automotive sectors, offers superior elasticity and resilience, making it suitable for applications like tires and medical products. In contrast, trans polyisoprene, though less common, is used in specialty applications where higher thermal stability and abrasion resistance are required. Both types play critical roles in various industries, influencing performance and application suitability.</p></p>
