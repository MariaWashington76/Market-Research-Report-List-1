<p><p>The open loop current sensor market can be divided into two main types: isolated and non-isolated sensors. Isolated current sensors provide electrical isolation between the monitored circuit and the sensing element, enhancing safety and reducing the risk of interference. Non-isolated sensors, on the other hand, do not offer this separation, typically resulting in simpler designs and lower costs. Both types are essential for various applications, including industrial automation, electric vehicles, and renewable energy systems, catering to different requirements for safety and performance.</p></p>
