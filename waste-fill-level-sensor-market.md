<p><p>The waste fill-level sensor market features two primary technologies: ultrasonic and laser sensors. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to measure the distance to the waste surface, offering a cost-effective solution for monitoring fill levels in various waste containers. In contrast, laser sensors employ light beams for precise measurements, providing higher accuracy and longer detection ranges. Both technologies are essential for efficient waste management, helping to optimize collection schedules and reduce operational costs by preventing overflows and unnecessary trips.</p></p>
